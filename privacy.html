Time Capture — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-14

Draft. This is the starter privacy policy shipped with Build 8. Before submitting to any app store or distributing publicly, replace this content with a final policy reviewed by counsel for your jurisdiction.

Summary

The Time Capture desktop app stores all your time-tracking data locally on your computer. We do not collect, transmit, or sell your activity data.

What we collect

When the Allow Announcement Checks setting is enabled (the default), the app periodically requests the announcements feed from our servers. That request includes an anonymous, randomly generated install_id and the app version number. The install ID lets us count active installs without identifying you; it cannot be linked back to a person, email address, or any of your time entries. Your task names, durations, and exports remain entirely on your device.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your task descriptions or any time-entry contents
  • Your name, email, or any personally identifying information
  • Your IP address (server logs may show it as part of standard web traffic, but it is not associated with the install ID for any user-tracking purpose)
  • Telemetry, crash reports, or diagnostic data — none

How to opt out

Open Settings and toggle off Allow Announcement Checks. With that off, the app makes no network requests at all.

Cached data

The announcement feed is cached locally so the inbox stays readable offline. You can clear it by uninstalling the app or by wiping the local database file.

Cloud / sync features (future)

If a future version of Time Capture adds optional cloud sync, that flow will be explicitly opt-in and covered by an updated privacy policy at that time. The standalone desktop app described here will always remain a local-first option.
